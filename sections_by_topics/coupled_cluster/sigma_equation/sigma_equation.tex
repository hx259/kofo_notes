\section{Sigma Equations}
\subsection{Amplitude Equations}
    The (UHF) residual equations which determine the cluster amplitudes are written as following:
    \begin{align}
        \sigma _{i}^{a} =& \langle \Phi _{i}^{a}|\hat{\mathcal{H}}|0\rangle = \langle 0 |\{\hat{i}^{\dagger}\hat{a}\}\hat{H}_{\text{N}}(1+ \hat{T}_{1} + \hat{T}_{2} + \hat{T}_{1}\hat{T}_{2}+\frac{1}{2!}\hat{T}_{1}^{2} + \frac{1}{3!}\hat{T}_{1}^{3}) |0\rangle _{\text{C}} \\
        \sigma _{ij}^{ab} =& \langle \Phi _{ij}^{ab}|\hat{\mathcal{H}}|0\rangle \nonumber \\
        =& \langle 0|\{\hat{i}^{\dagger}\hat{j}^{\dagger}\hat{b}\hat{a}\}\hat{H}_{\text{N}}(1+\hat{T}_{1}+\hat{T}_{2}+\hat{T}_{1}\hat{T}_{2} + \frac{1}{2!}\hat{T}_{1}^{2} + \frac{1}{2!}\hat{T}_{2}^{2} + \frac{1}{2!}\hat{T}_{1}^{2}\hat{T}_{2} + \frac{1}{3!}\hat{T}_{1}^{3} + \frac{1}{4!}\hat{T}_{1}^{4})|0 \rangle _{\text{C}}
    \end{align}
    For the $\sigma _{i}^{a}$ equation, the $e ^{\hat{T}}$ operator expands up to (disconnected) triple excitations, due to the two-body natural of $\hat{H}_{\text{N}}$ operator; and similarly the $\sigma _{ij}^{ab}$ equation includes up to (disconnected) quadruple excitations.
    \\
    As for the RHF residual equations, the singlet spin-traced operators $E _{i}^{a}$ and $e _{ij}^{ab}$ need to be used.
